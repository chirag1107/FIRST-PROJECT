ABOUT

The Amazing
Spider-Man

Spider-Man was created by writer-editor Stan Lee and writer-artist Steve Ditko. He first appeared in Amazing Fantasy #15, published by Marvel Comics in 1962 — a high-school student who gained spider-like superpowers after being bitten by a radioactive spider.

"With great power comes great responsibility." — this principle guides Peter Parker as New York City's masked protector.

HISTORY

Spider-Man Through the Ages

Six decades of legacy — from a comic book panel to a multiverse phenomenon

1962

The Birth of Spider-Man

Peter Parker first appeared in Amazing Fantasy #15, created by Stan Lee and Steve Ditko. A radioactive spider bite changed a shy science nerd into the world's greatest superhero.

🕸 Comic Origin
1977

Live-Action TV Debut

Spider-Man made his first live-action appearance on CBS Television. Nicholas Hammond portrayed Peter Parker, bringing the web-slinger to millions of American homes.

📺 Television
2002

Sam Raimi's Trilogy Begins

Tobey Maguire swings onto the big screen in Sam Raimi's blockbuster that redefined superhero cinema. The trilogy grossed over $2.5 billion worldwide and cemented Spider-Man as a cultural icon.

🎬 Cinema
2012

The Amazing Spider-Man

Andrew Garfield took on the mantle, bringing a new era with a fresh emotional take on Peter Parker's origins. A darker, more complex portrayal resonated with a new generation of fans.

🎥 Reboot Era
2016

MCU Integration

Tom Holland debuted as Spider-Man in Captain America: Civil War, bringing Spidey into the Marvel Cinematic Universe and a whole new universe of possibilities and heroic team-ups.

🦸 MCU
2021

No Way Home – Multiverse

All three Spider-Men unite in the most epic crossover event in superhero history. No Way Home broke box office records worldwide and became a cultural phenomenon.

Doctor Octopus

Otto Gunther Octavius

A brilliant nuclear physicist whose lab accident fused four mechanical arms to his body, granting him fearsome cybernetic power. He founded the Sinister Six and remains one of Spider-Man's most dangerous and intellectual adversaries.
